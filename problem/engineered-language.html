Sometimes the word comes before the thing.

You are part of an Iron Age tribe. You're in the woods when you notice a pattern of stripes. You've seen that pattern before. It belongs to the thing that ate one of your cousins. You sprint back to the village and desperately try to tell people about it. You definitely need a way to refer to it.

🐯
"Tiger"

This is the organic development of language: first there is a pattern, out there, in the world. Then it is named.

Deliberate language engineering works the reverse way. Instead of language arising as a response to the need to pick out pre-existing patterns, names and words are deliberately created, introduced, and spread in order to shape how existing patterns are perceived, interpreted, and discussed. Or even to try to bootstrap new patterns into existence wholesale.

Some of this is benign. 

But some of it is the result of political struggle over shared maps proceeding by way of engineering names, words, and concepts.
