Problem Solution (Upcoming) Map

Social life depends on maps that overlap across many minds.

A map  is a mental representation of reality. That map is "shared" when several people use the same concepts, words, symbols, and distinctions that that map is made of.

When that happens, when there is sufficient overlap between maps, communication and coordination are made possible, creating a shared social life. We start inhabiting a shared social world.

Social reality, thus, depends heavily on shared maps. Maps that are transmitted through public language: words and symbols allowing concepts to spread across minds and overlapping concepts allowing overlapping maps to emerge.

This is why shaping public language can shape social reality. Whoever can shift the shared maps — by introducing a new concept, relabeling an existing category, or changing what something feels like — can shift what counts as real, legitimate, or worth acting on.
