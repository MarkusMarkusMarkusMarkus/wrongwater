The power of social reality is that it determines what is treated as real.

Consider a bank run. What is a bank run? It's what happens when enough people stop treating a bank as safe and start acting on that changed belief. And then, suddenly, the bank is in trouble. A shift in shared maps has immediate material consequences.

Shared maps help determine not just which banks remain liquid but which companies are viable, which institutions retain legitimacy, who counts as a ruler, who the president is, who commands the army, and who gets obeyed. Social reality is not separate from power. It helps organize authority, compliance, and coordination.

It is not an accident that revolutionaries so often seize television stations, radio towers, and now social media channels. Power depends not only on force, but on controlling the shared maps through which force, legitimacy, and authority are recognized.

That is why struggles over public language, symbols, and shared belief are never merely symbolic. They are struggles over the map — and therefore over the social world organized through it.

legitimacy
Legitimacy is a social equilibrium, not a private opinion.

Authority is the socially recognized capacity to direct action. Legitimacy is a stable structure of interlocking expectations that such authority is rightful.

A ruler is not legitimate merely because some individuals privately approve of them. They are legitimate when enough people regard their authority as rightful, expect others to regard it as such, and act accordingly. These higher-order expectations help stabilize obedience, deference, and coordination.

Which is why legitimacy can persist even after private belief has begun to erode. People may stop personally believing in a ruler's rightfulness while continuing to comply, because they still expect others to comply. What holds the system together is often not conviction alone, but the expectation of others' conviction and behavior.

That is also why legitimacy can collapse so quickly. Once mutual expectations begin to shift, public behavior can change with startling speed.
